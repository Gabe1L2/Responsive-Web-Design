Here's a timeline of MF DOOM's life

  • 1971 - MF DOOM (born Dumile Daniel) is born in London, England.
  • 1978 - MF DOOM and his family move to Long Island, New York, after his parents immigrate from the Caribbean.
  • 1993 - MF DOOM starts his career in the underground rap scene as Zev Love X, part of the group KMD.
  • 1993 - KMD's second album, Black Bastards, is completed but never officially released due to the group's disbandment after the death of member DJ Subroc.
  • 1996 - MF DOOM re-emerges as a solo artist, adopting the "DOOM" persona, wearing a metal mask, and performing under the name MF DOOM.
  • 1999 - DOOM releases his debut solo album Operation: Doomsday, marking his official return to the music scene.
  • 2002 - DOOM releases Take Me to Your Leader, his first album as the character King Geedorah, under the independent label Big Dada.
  • 2004 - MF DOOM collaborates with producer Madlib to release the critically acclaimed album Madvillainy under the name Madvillain.
  • 2004 - DOOM also collaborates with MF Grimm on The Downfall of Ibliys: A Ghetto Opera.
  • 2005 - He releases Mmm... Food, an album that becomes a cult classic in underground hip-hop.
  • 2006 - MF DOOM collaborates with producer Danger Mouse for the album The Mouse and the Mask, a collaboration with the Adult Swim network.
  • 2009 - DOOM collaborates with Jneiro Jarel for the album Born Like This, showcasing a new, experimental sound.
  • 2010 - He releases Key to the Kuffs with producer Jneiro Jarel, under the alias JJ DOOM.
  • 2014 - MF DOOM performs live shows occasionally but remains elusive, maintaining a mysterious aura around his career.
  • 2020 - MF DOOM passes away on October 31, 2020, though the news was publicly announced in December, shocking fans worldwide.
